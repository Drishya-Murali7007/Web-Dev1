The Sun is the star at the centre of the Solar System. It is a massive, nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma, heated to incandescence by nuclear fusion reactions in its core, radiating the energy from its surface mainly as visible light and infrared radiation with 10% at ultraviolet energies.



Rigel is the name of a bright blue supergiant star in the constellation Orion, located in the lower-left corner of the constellation as viewed from the Northern Hemisphere. It is the seventh brightest star in the night sky, known for its blue-white color and high temperature.

Betelgeuse is a massive, reddish red supergiant star in the Orion constellation, known for its variable brightness and distinct color. It is one of the brightest stars in the night sky. In 2020, its "Great Dimming" event was attributed to a giant ejection of gas that condensed into dust.
